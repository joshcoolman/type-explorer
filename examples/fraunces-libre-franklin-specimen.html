07In context

The pairing, proven on the page

A display serif carries the voice; an even sans does the patient work of the paragraph. Set them together and the page reads as considered, not loud.

Pairing a characterful serif with a neutral sans is one of the oldest moves in typography, and it endures because the two faces are doing genuinely different jobs. The headline wants personality and contrast; the body wants to disappear into legibility.

Fraunces supplies the contrast and the slightly antique warmth that makes a headline feel authored. Its optical-size axis lets the same family run from delicate text to a heavy, high-impact display cut without ever changing fonts.

Libre Franklin answers with a calm, modern grotesque that holds its color across a full column. The result is a layout with a clear hierarchy and a single, coherent voice — exactly what an editorial spread is after.
