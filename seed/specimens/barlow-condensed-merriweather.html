Margins and mastheads

Notes on print, pixels, and the space between them.

A design journal needs column heads that sit tight and tall, and reading that carries quietly underneath. The condensed display sets dense, confident titles; the serif body keeps long passages comfortable and authoritative.

It is a workhorse pairing for editorial: economical up top, warm and steady below. Captions, pull quotes, and running text all find their place without fighting for attention.
