A walk-in parlor on the east side of the river. Hand-poked tradition, electric machines, and a no-flinch policy. Closed Mondays. Cash, courage, and an idea — that's all you need to bring.

The shop sits two doors down from a biker bar that's been pouring since before the bridge was painted. Neon bleeds across the bricks at night and the buzz of the needle answers the jukebox. Regulars know the drill: sign the waiver, pick your poison, sit still.

Our artists cut their teeth on old-school flash — eagles, daggers, roses with thorns that mean it. We do fine-line work too, but we won't pretend it's precious. A good tattoo earns its place. It should look like a decision you made on purpose and would make again.

Bring a reference or bring a story. We'll draw it up, talk it through, and put it on you straight. No upsell, no judgment, no second-guessing once the stencil's down. The ink is permanent. So is the memory. That's the whole point of the thing.

Oswald is a reworking of the classic gothic typeface style, redrawn for the screen. Its narrow set and tall caps were built for headlines and signage, but the variable weight keeps it readable down to caption size — perfect for price lists, aftercare cards, and the fine print on the consent form nobody reads but everybody signs.
