07In context

Sixty minutes, one steady breath at a time

Our heated flow meets the city's cool mornings. Arrive as you are; leave a little lighter. Bitter carries the headline, Nunito carries everything you actually have to read before class.

The studio sits two flights above Valencia Street, where the fog burns off around eleven and the windows go gold. We keep the room at ninety-two degrees, the playlist low, and the schedule honest. No mirrors, no leaderboard, no rush — just a teacher counting you through the breath while the streetcar rattles past below.

Beginners are not an afterthought here. Every week opens with a slow foundations class where we name the shapes, build the transitions, and let you fall out of a pose without anyone noticing. By Friday the same sequence moves like water, and the thing you could not do on Monday feels like it was always yours.

Membership is month to month because trust should be too. Drop in when the week allows, book a private when you want detail, or simply sit in the back and breathe. The mat is forty inches of quiet that belongs to you for one hour. Bring water, bring nothing else, and let the rest of San Francisco wait at the door.
